Alfajores

Alfajores photo by my amazing wife

Description

Alfajores are soft, delicate cookies filled with dulce de leche and rolled in coconut. They are popular in many South American countries.

This recipe comes from my wife’s Brazilian kitchen. She bakes them, I just eat them

Ingredients

  • all-purpose flour 200 gram
  • cornstarch 300 gram
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • unsalted butter 200 gram (at room temperature)
  • granulated sugar or powdered sugar 150 gram
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • dulce de leche for filling 350 gram
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened shredded coconut

Steps

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, cornstarch, baking powder, and baking soda. Set the dry mixture aside.
  2. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, beat the butter and sugar on medium speed until pale and fluffy, about 3 minutes. Add the egg yolks and vanilla and mix just until combined.
  3. Turn the mixer to low and slowly add the dry ingredients. Mix only until the dough comes together. If it feels too dry or crumbly, add a tiny splash of cold water or milk, a little at a time, until it holds together. Avoid overmixing so the cookies stay tender.
  4. Shape the dough into a ball, then press it into a flat disc. Wrap it in plastic wrap and chill in the fridge for 1–2 hours, or until firm enough to roll out.
  5. If you’re making the dough ahead, you can keep it in the fridge for up to 3 days or freeze it for up to 1 month. Thaw frozen dough overnight in the fridge before using.
  6. Take the dough out of the fridge and let it rest on the counter for a few minutes so it softens slightly. On a lightly floured surface (or between two sheets of baking paper), roll it out to about 3–5 mm (⅛–¼ inch) thick. Cut out circles with a 5 cm (2 inch) round or fluted cutter and place them on baking trays lined with parchment.
  7. If the dough gets too soft while you’re working, pop it back into the fridge for a short chill. Gather and re-roll the scraps as needed. Place the trays with the cut cookies in the fridge or freezer for at least 15 minutes so they hold their shape in the oven.
  8. Preheat the oven to 177°C. Bake the cookies for 7–10 minutes, or until the edges just start to turn golden. Let them cool on the tray for about 10 minutes, then move them carefully to a wire rack to cool completely.
  9. Once the cookies are cool, spread about a teaspoon of dulce de leche on the bottom of half of them. Top with the remaining cookies to make sandwiches, gently pressing so a little caramel squeezes out around the edges. Roll the sticky edges in shredded coconut.
  10. Store the finished alfajores in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 1 week, or freeze for up to 2 months. To thaw frozen cookies, leave them covered on the counter or in the fridge overnight.
